What It Does

Turn a $5 ESP32 into a portable surveillance device detector.

BLE & WiFi Scanning

Dual-protocol scanning detects surveillance devices by matching MAC address OUI prefixes against a database of known vendors.

LED Proximity Alerts

Built-in LED flashes based on RSSI signal strength — rapid red when close, slow pulse when distant. No extra hardware needed.

PWA Dashboard

Install the web interface on your phone — no app store required. Real-time device list, GPS tagging, and configuration from any browser.

GeoJSON & CSV Export

Export geotagged detections as GeoJSON for mapping or CSV for analysis. Visualize on geojson.io or contribute to DeFlock.me.

How It Works

From unboxing to detecting surveillance devices in three steps.

1

Flash the ESP32

Upload the firmware using Arduino IDE or PlatformIO. Select your board, set partition scheme to "Default 4MB with spiffs", and flash.

2

Connect to the AP

Power on the ESP32. Join WiFi network "OUI-spy-TOO" and open 192.168.4.1 in your browser. Install as a PWA on your phone.

3

Detect & Export

Grant location permission. Surveillance devices appear in real time. Export as GeoJSON or CSV to map them on DeFlock.me, geojson.io, or Ushahidi.

LED Behavior

Pattern Meaning
Slow pulse (green)Idle, scanning
Blue flashActive BLE scan
Rapid red flashSurveillance device very close
Medium red flashDevice at medium range
Slow red flashDevice detected (far)
Rainbow sequenceStartup (RGB LED boards)
