## LEGAL BASIS

This request is made under the **Washington Public Records Act (RCW 42.56)**.

Relevant legal context:
- **Carpenter v. United States (2018)** — The US Supreme Court held that accessing cell-site location data constitutes a search requiring a warrant. Purchasing equivalent data from commercial brokers raises the same constitutional concerns.
- **Washington Constitution, Article I, Section 7** — "No person shall be disturbed in his private affairs, or his home invaded, without authority of law." Washington courts have interpreted this more broadly than the federal Fourth Amendment.
- **RCW 10.93.160 (Shield Law)** — Prohibits Washington agencies from using resources for immigration enforcement, which may be implicated if data broker tools are used for that purpose.
