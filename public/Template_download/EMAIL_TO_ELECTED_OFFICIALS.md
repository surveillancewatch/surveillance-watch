## EMAIL 1: TO YOUR STATE LEGISLATOR

**To:** [State Senator/Representative email — find at leg.wa.gov]
**Subject:** Request for Surveillance Oversight Legislation — Constituent from [YOUR DISTRICT]

```
Dear [Senator/Representative LAST NAME],

I am writing as your constituent from [YOUR CITY] to request your
support for surveillance technology oversight legislation in
Washington State.

THE ISSUE:
Over 80 cities in Washington currently deploy automated surveillance
technologies — license plate readers, facial recognition, cell-site
simulators, and data broker contracts — often without public
knowledge, community input, or meaningful oversight.

A University of Washington Center for Human Rights report (October
2025) found that at least 18 Washington agencies had unauthorized
federal access to local surveillance data, potentially violating
our state's Shield Law (RCW 10.93.160).

In November 2025, a Skagit County Superior Court ruled that
surveillance camera data is public record (Rodriguez v. City of
Sedro Woolley), establishing important precedent. But more
legislative action is needed.

MY REQUEST:
I ask that you support legislation that would:

1. Require public notice and community input BEFORE surveillance
   technology is deployed by any government agency
2. Mandate regular audits of surveillance data-sharing agreements,
   particularly those involving federal agencies
3. Establish data minimization requirements — agencies should only
   collect and retain data necessary for specific, documented
   purposes
4. Strengthen enforcement of Washington's Shield Law to prevent
   surveillance data from being used for immigration enforcement
5. Require transparency reports on surveillance technology use,
   costs, and effectiveness

WHY THIS MATTERS:
Surveillance technology affects every Washington resident. Whether
driving to work, attending a community event, or simply walking
down the street, our movements and activities are being recorded
and stored. Without oversight, this data can be misused, shared
without authorization, or used to target vulnerable communities.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss this further. Please
let me know your position on surveillance oversight legislation.

Thank you for your service to our community.

Sincerely,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
[YOUR PHONE]
```

## EMAIL 2: TO YOUR CITY COUNCIL

**To:** [City Council member or full council email]
**Subject:** Request for Local Surveillance Oversight — [YOUR CITY] Resident

```
Dear [Council Member NAME / City Council],

I am a resident of [YOUR CITY] writing to request that our city
establish oversight and transparency requirements for surveillance
technology.

WHAT I'VE LEARNED:
Through public records research, I've learned that government
agencies across Washington use surveillance technologies including
automated license plate readers, facial recognition, and data
broker services — often without public awareness or formal
policies governing their use.

A November 2025 court ruling (Rodriguez v. City of Sedro Woolley)
confirmed that this surveillance data is public record. A
University of Washington report found that many agencies were
unaware of unauthorized federal access to their surveillance data.

MY REQUEST:
I ask the [CITY NAME] City Council to:

1. DISCLOSE what surveillance technologies our city currently uses
   or has access to, including vendor names and contract costs
2. ADOPT a surveillance oversight ordinance requiring:
   - Public notice before new surveillance technology is acquired
   - Community impact assessments
   - Annual transparency reports
   - Clear data retention and deletion policies
   - Restrictions on sharing data with federal agencies
3. AUDIT existing surveillance contracts and data-sharing
   agreements for compliance with Washington's Shield Law

Cities like Oakland, San Francisco, and others have adopted
surveillance oversight ordinances. [CITY NAME] should join them
in ensuring that technology serves the public interest with
appropriate accountability.

I plan to attend an upcoming council meeting to speak about this
issue during public comment. I welcome the opportunity to discuss
this further.

Thank you for your attention to this important matter.

Sincerely,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
[YOUR PHONE]
```

## EMAIL 3: TO YOUR US SENATOR OR REPRESENTATIVE

**To:** [Find at congress.gov or 5calls.org]
**Subject:** Support Federal Data Privacy Legislation — Constituent from [YOUR STATE/DISTRICT]

```
Dear [Senator/Representative LAST NAME],

I am writing as your constituent from [YOUR CITY], Washington to
urge you to support comprehensive federal data privacy legislation.

THE PROBLEM:
The United States remains one of the few developed nations without
a comprehensive federal data privacy law. This gap enables:

- Government agencies purchasing personal data from commercial
  brokers to bypass warrant requirements
- Companies like Palantir, Babel Street, and Thomson Reuters
  selling location data, financial records, and social media
  activity to ICE, DHS, and local law enforcement
- No meaningful restrictions on how long personal data is retained
  or who can access it
- No requirement for agencies to disclose what surveillance
  technologies they use or what data they purchase

IN WASHINGTON STATE:
Over 80 cities deploy automated surveillance cameras. A University
of Washington report found unauthorized federal access to local
surveillance data. Residents have no way to know what personal
data the government holds about them or how it's being used.

MY REQUEST:
I urge you to support legislation that would:

1. Require warrants for government purchase of personal data from
   commercial brokers
2. Establish data minimization requirements for all government
   surveillance programs
3. Create transparency requirements for surveillance technology
   procurement and use
4. Give individuals the right to know what personal data the
   government holds about them
5. Establish meaningful penalties for misuse of surveillance data

Americans deserve to know when and how their government is
watching them. Please support comprehensive privacy protections.

Thank you for your service.

Sincerely,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
[YOUR PHONE]
```

## TIPS FOR EFFECTIVE EMAILS

- **Use the subject line wisely** — Mention you're a constituent and the topic
- **Keep it concise** — One page or less
- **Be specific** — Name technologies, cite facts, make clear asks
- **Personalize** — Add a sentence about why this matters to you specifically
- **Follow up** — If you don't hear back in 2 weeks, send a polite follow-up
- **Share** — Encourage others in your community to send similar emails
